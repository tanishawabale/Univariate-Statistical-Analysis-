# Univariate-Statistical-Analysis-
**HISTOGRAM INTERPRETATION**
**1. Age – Histogram Interpretation**
  The age distribution shows a higher concentration of individuals between 18–25 years, with frequency gradually decreasing for older age groups.
  The distribution does not show strong skewness but slightly favors lower age groups.
**2. BMI – Histogram Interpretation**
  The BMI distribution is approximately normal (bell-shaped), with most values concentrated between 25 and 35.
**3. Children – Histogram Interpretation**
  The number of children distribution is positively skewed. Most individuals have 0, 1, or 2 children. This suggests that smaller family sizes are more common in the dataset.
**4. Charges – Histogram Interpretation**
  The medical charges distribution is highly positively skewed (right-skewed). Most individuals have lower medical charges, while a smaller number of individuals have very high charges.
**KDE INTERPRETATION**
**1. KDE plot of Age**
  The age distribution shows higher density between 18–25 and 40–55 years.
**2. KDE plot of BMI**
  BMI shows an approximately normal distribution, centered around 30.
**3. KDE plot of Children**
  The highest density is at 0 , and density decreases as the number of children increases. This shows most individuals have fewer children.
**4. KDE plot of Charges**
  The charges distribution is positively skewed, with most individuals having lower charges and fewer individuals having very high medical expenses.
**BOXPLOT INTERPRETATION**
**1. Boxplot of Age**
  The median age is around 39 years, with most individuals between 27 and 51 years. No significant outliers are present.
**2. Boxplot of BMI**
  The median BMI is around 30, with most values between 26 and 35. The data shows no significant outliers.
**3. Boxplot of Children**
  The median number of children is 1. Most people have between 0 and 2 children. The values range from 0 to 5, with no significant outliers.
**4. Boxplot of Charges**
  The median insurance charge is around 9,000–10,000. Most charges lie between about 5,000 and 17,000. The data shows a wide range, with some individuals having very high charges up to around 35,000.
**COUNTPLOT INTERPRETATION**
**1. Countplot of Sex**
  The dataset contains a nearly equal number of males and females, indicating no gender imbalance.
**2. Countplot of Smokers**
  The majority of individuals are non-smokers (0), while a much smaller proportion are smokers (1), indicating non-smokers dominate the dataset.
**3. Countplot of Region**
  The number of individuals is almost evenly distributed across all regions, with region 2 having slightly more observations than the others.
